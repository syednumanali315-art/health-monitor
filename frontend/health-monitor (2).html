Emergency Advice
✅ What To Do
Call your doctor immediately
Call ambulance (108) if condition is severe
Keep the patient calm and comfortable
Check breathing rate and pulse
Give prescribed emergency medicine if available
Inform family members immediately
Keep patient in safe, ventilated space
❌ What NOT To Do
Do not panic — stay calm and act quickly
Do not ignore warning signs or delay
Do not give random medicine without prescription
Do not leave patient alone in severe emergency
Do not delay calling emergency services
Do not move patient if neck/spine injury suspected
Do not give food/water if patient is unconscious
AWS SNS Emergency Alert Configuration
Configure who receives emergency alerts. In production, these connect to your AWS SNS topic.
Backend SNS Code (Node.js)
const {SNSClient,PublishCommand}=require("@aws-sdk/client-sns");
const sns=new SNSClient({region:process.env.AWS_REGION});
async function sendAlert(msg,patientId){
  await sns.send(new PublishCommand({
    TopicArn:process.env.SNS_TOPIC_ARN,
    Message:`EMERGENCY: ${msg} | Patient: ${patientId}`,
    Subject:"Health Emergency Alert"
  }));
}

Heart Rate Logic
Adults: <60 Low · 60–100 Normal · >100 High
Newborn: Normal 70–190 bpm
Infant: Normal 80–160 bpm
Toddler: Normal 80–130 bpm
Child: Normal 70–120 bpm
Teen: Normal 60–100 bpm
Temperature & Other Logic
<95°F Low Temperature
97–99°F Normal
99–100.4°F Mild Fever
>100.4°F High Fever · ≥100 = Fever Detected
>102°F High Risk / Emergency
O₂: ≥95 Normal · 90–94 Low · <90 Critical
Sugar: 70–140 Normal · >140 High · <70 Low
